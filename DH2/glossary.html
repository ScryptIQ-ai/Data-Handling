Data Handling 2 - Glossary

Glossary DH2
Anti-correlation: A negative relationship between two variables where high values in one variable correspond to low values in another, resulting in a correlation coefficient close to -1.
Bivariate analysis: Statistical analysis that involves examining the relationship between two variables to identify patterns, correlations, or dependencies.
Colorbar: A visual scale displayed alongside a heatmap or image plot that maps colors to numerical values, helping interpret the magnitude of data points.
Correlation: A statistical measure that describes the strength and direction of a relationship between two variables, indicating how they change together.
Correlation coefficient: A numerical value between -1 and 1 that quantifies the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables.
Correlation matrix: A table showing correlation coefficients between all possible pairs of variables in a dataset, resulting in an N×N matrix for N variables.
Covariance: An unnormalised measure of how two variables change together, similar to correlation but dependent on the scale of the data.
Dataset properties: Characteristics and features of a dataset including its dimensions, statistical measures, data types, and relationships between variables.
Heatmap: A graphical representation of data where individual values in a matrix are represented as colors, commonly used to visualise correlation matrices.
Independent variables: Variables that show no systematic relationship with each other, where knowing one provides no information about the other.
Monotonic relationship: A relationship between variables where they consistently move in the same direction (or opposite directions) but not necessarily at a constant rate.
Multivariate analysis: Statistical analysis involving more than two variables simultaneously to understand complex relationships within a dataset.
Normalisation: The process of scaling data to a standard range or distribution, often used to make different variables comparable.
NumPy array: A fundamental data structure in Python for numerical computing that stores homogeneous data in a grid format, optimised for mathematical operations.
Pearson correlation coefficient (PCC): A measure of linear correlation between two variables, calculated as the covariance divided by the product of their standard deviations.
Relationship: A pattern or connection between variables where changes in one variable are associated with changes in another.
Scatter plot: A visualisation that displays values for two variables as points on a coordinate system, used to identify relationships and patterns.
Similarity: The degree to which two variables or datasets resemble each other in their patterns or values.
Spearman correlation: A rank-based correlation measure that assesses monotonic relationships between variables, not limited to linear relationships like Pearson.
Statistical significance: A determination of whether an observed relationship or pattern is unlikely to have occurred by chance alone.
Symmetric matrix: A matrix where values are mirrored across the diagonal, such as correlation matrices where the correlation of A with B equals B with A.
Undirected measure: A statistical measure where the order of comparison doesn't matter; the measure of A relative to B equals the measure of B relative to A.
Univariate analysis: Statistical analysis that examines a single variable at a time, focusing on its distribution and characteristics in isolation.
