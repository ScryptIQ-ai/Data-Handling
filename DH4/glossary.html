Data Handling 4 - Glossary

Glossary DH4
Time series data: Data points indexed in time order.
Sampling rate (sr): The number of samples recorded per unit of time (e.g., per second).
Period: The duration of a recording or cycle.
Channels (Sensors): Individual sources of data, such as electrodes in an EEG recording.
Electroencephalogram (EEG): A recording of electrical activity of the brain.
Absence seizure: A type of seizure involving brief, sudden lapses of consciousness.
Input arguments: Variables passed into a function.
Outputs: Values returned by a function.
Docstring: Documentation within a function explaining its operation and arguments.
Frequency: The number of cycles a waveform completes per unit of time (e.g., Hz).
Filter: A process to remove unwanted components (frequencies) from a signal.
Band-pass filter: A filter that passes frequencies within a certain range and rejects frequencies outside that range.
Alpha band: Neural oscillations in the frequency range of 8–13 Hz.
Delta band: Neural oscillations in the frequency range of 1–4 Hz.
Fourier spectrum: A decomposition of a time series into a sum of sine waves of different frequencies.
Fourier Transform: A mathematical transform that decomposes a function (often time-domain) into its constituent frequencies (frequency-domain).
Stationary time series: A time series whose statistical properties (mean, variance) do not change over time.
Harmonics: Integer multiples of a fundamental frequency.
Fundamental frequency: The lowest frequency of a periodic waveform.
Band power: The total power of a signal within a specific frequency range.
Cross-correlation matrix: A matrix showing the correlation coefficients between multiple variables (time series).
Univariate features: Features derived from a single variable.
Bivariate features: Features derived from the relationship between two variables.
